# Sycren — Bootstrap Foundation Roadmap
> Strategic Execution Plan — Bootstrapped from Zero
> June 2026 — Confidential to Founders

---

## Executive Summary

Sycren is born with an ambitious vision: a unified platform integrating software development, cloud hosting, domain management and cybersecurity. The founding document v1.0 correctly describes the final destination. This roadmap defines the realistic path to reach it starting from the actual conditions: zero capital, zero VAT number, two sixteen-year-old founders.

The legal and commercial structure will be activated when the first founder reaches legal age. In the preceding period, every activity is oriented toward building the product, the brand and the network — so that by the time of formal opening, Sycren is already ready to operate at full capacity.

The guiding principle is one:

> **Build everything now. Sell at the right time. Structure follows revenue, not the other way around.**

## Operational Choice — Solution B: Wait for Legal Age

Both founders are 16 years old with no VAT number. Without a trusted adult available to assume the role of legal representative, the only viable path is to wait until one of the founders turns 18 before starting to invoice clients.

This choice directly impacts the roadmap structure: the period until legal age is not wasted time — it is the time to build everything that will make the commercial launch immediate and effective.

### What can be done legally before 18
- ✅ Develop product and platform
- ✅ Build the brand and digital presence
- ✅ Network, acquire contacts and pre-qualify potential clients
- ✅ Collect letters of intent (LOI) or informal agreements with future clients
- ✅ Publish technical content, build authority in the industry
- ✅ Participate in hackathons and competitions (prizes managed through parents)
- ✅ Contribute to open source projects to increase visibility
- ✅ Create profiles on Upwork/Contra (with parent account if needed)

### What cannot be done before 18 (without a guardian)
- ❌ Sign binding commercial contracts
- ❌ Issue invoices
- ❌ Open business bank accounts
- ❌ Establish an SRL or other corporate form
- ❌ Integrate Stripe for live payments

### Time Estimate
If Angelo or Cosimo turns 18 within the next 12-24 months, the plan is fully executable. The first to reach that age becomes the operational legal representative until the formal opening of the company, which includes both partners.

### Module 2 — `sycren-db` Database Foundation — 🕐 16 years
**Estimate: Week 2-4** | **Priority: HIGH (blocking)**

**Why second:** shared package with Prisma schema, migrations, TypeScript types and DB utilities for ALL applications. Without this, no other module can be built.

**Database architecture:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                     sycren-db                            │
│                                                         │
│  Prisma Schema (PostgreSQL via Supabase)                │
│  ├── User (id, email, name, role, passwordHash, 2fa)   │
│  ├── Project (id, name, status, budget, clientId)      │
│  ├── Invoice (id, amount, status, dueDate, projectId)  │
│  ├── Domain (id, name, tld, expiryDate, dnsRecords)    │
│  ├── Server (id, cpu, ram, disk, provider, status)     │
│  ├── SecurityRule (id, type, config, serverId)         │
│  ├── Notification (id, type, message, userId, read)    │
│  └── WaitlistEntry (id, email, name, projectType, msg) │
│                                                         │
│  MongoDB (secondary) — logs, configurations, sessions   │
│  Redis (cache) — session store, rate limiting, pub/sub  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Deliverables:**
- Centralized Prisma schema in `packages/sycren-db`
- Versioned migrations (PostgreSQL on Supabase)
- Generated and published TypeScript types
- DB utilities (connection singleton, CRUD helpers, seed data)
- Published as private npm package (GitHub Packages)

**Completion criteria:**

## Critical Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Impact | Probability | Correct Alternative |
|---|---|---|---|
| Waiting for legal age to build the product | Launch delayed by 6-12 months | HIGH | Build everything now, sell later |
| Not collecting contacts in Phase 0 | Cold launch, zero pipeline | MEDIUM | Waitlist + contact form from day 1 |
| Building everything without priorities | Waste on non-critical features | HIGH | Follow the order: Landing → DB → Studio → App → Cloud → Domains → Security |
| Cosimo doesn't build portfolio | 0 technical credibility at launch | MEDIUM | At least 4 public repos by Month 2 |
| Opening SRL before revenue | Fixed costs €1k-3k with no income | LOW | SRL only at MRR > €3,000 |
| Targeting only the Italian market | Low pricing, high competition | MEDIUM | International as default (EUR/USD) |
| Not documenting anything | Chaos when scaling | HIGH | Every repo has README + requirements + todo |
| Pricing too low | Undervaluation, burnout | MEDIUM | Start from €500 (landing) and raise after 3 clients |
| Not testing payments | Blocked on first sale | LOW | Test Stripe in test mode before launch |

## Closing Notes

Choosing Solution B means turning the age constraint into a strategic advantage: the pre-commercial period becomes a time of total construction, without the distraction of client management. When Sycren opens for sales, it will already be a mature product with a pipeline of potential clients, a positioned brand and two founders with a solid technical portfolio.

Many of the most relevant tech companies of the last twenty years were founded by people who built for months or years before having their first paying client. The difference is that they had no choice. You do — and you are using it the right way.

The sequence remains unchanged:

```
1. Build  →  2. Position  →  3. Collect contacts
4. Wait for the legal moment  →  5. Sell  →  6. Scale
```
